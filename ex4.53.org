#+title: Exercise 4.53

The result of evaluating

#+begin_src scheme
  (let ((pairs '()))
    (if-fail
     (let ((p (prime-sum-pair '(1 3 5 8)
                              '(20 35 110))))
       (permanent-set! pairs (cons p pairs))
       (amb))
     pairs))
#+end_src

is the empty list ~'()~.

The definition of ~prime-sum-pair~ for reference:

#+begin_src scheme
  (define (prime-sum-pair list1 list2)
    (let ((a (an-element-of list1))
          (b (an-element-of list2)))
      (require (prime? (+ a b)))
      (list a b)))
#+end_src

This is because the sum of the first element of both lists, 21, is not
a prime number. Thus, the ~require~ expression in ~prime-sum-pair~ causes
the failure of the ~let~ expression. This in turn causes the ~if-fail~
expression to evaluate its second argument, which is still the empty
list.
